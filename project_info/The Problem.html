The Problem

Picture this: A thriving cannabis dispensary, bustling with customers, but plagued by a critical issue - the inability to process card payments. The legal cannabis industry in the United States is currently operating as an entirely cash-based business model, posing significant challenges for 8000 retailers and a substantial customer base of around 60 million people. In 2024, these customers spent an estimated $33 billion on cash-only cannabis purchases, leading to a range of issues for the industry's retailers. Cannabis is still highly regulated despite growing state legalization. Cannabis is legal in the United States by state law but remains illegal under federal law — and since the federal government oversees all US banks and credit unions —and whilst there is no specific law preventing them from doing so, major banks and credit card networks choose not to get involved in the legal cannabis industry. Traditional banks backed by the Federal Reserve continue to shut their doors to state-licensed cannabis companies simply because they are unwilling to touch funds that have passed through a federally challenged industry. For years, cannabis businesses have been trapped in a cash-only paradigm, facing challenges that would make any entrepreneur's head spin:
Security nightmares : Imagine managing millions in cash, always at risk of theft.
: Cash-only means smaller transactions and missed opportunities.
Compliance headaches: Tracking and reporting cash transactions is a regulatory minefield. Consumers cannot purchase products with their debit or credit card. They need to come up with cash, and they'll most likely withdraw it from the illegal ATM installed in the store and pay through the nose for the privilege. It also excludes them from loyalty programs and spending rewards as there is no way of tracking their spending habits. This issue affects not only the consumer but also retailers, who continue to face higher risks associated with all-cash operations. Dispensaries encounter major complications when it comes to processing payments, and they hate having to handle cash. Since banking falls under federal laws and guidelines, payment processing in dispensaries is a major problem. Visa/MasterCard recently doubled down on that stance by publicly announcing that their branded cards cannot be used to pay for cannabis products. Dispensaries run security risks by keeping a lot of cash on hand. There was a considerable uptick in dispensary robberies last year, largely because criminals know there's a ton of cash on-site for the taking. That’s only one of the problems. Retailers encounter further problems involving processing, bookkeeping, accounting, payroll, securing cash, transporting cash, and sales losses. This has created a huge gap in the market and made it very difficult for cannabis retailers to operate efficiently.
