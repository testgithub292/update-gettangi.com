Understanding the Problem

Understanding the Banking and Payments Landscape

To understand the problem, you first need to know how business banking and card payments work. For traditional businesses like grocery stores, coffee shops, and restaurants, obtaining banking and card payment processing is a breeze. In fact, these businesses are often approved instantly, showcasing the efficiency of the system. High-risk sectors, such as gaming and gambling, face unique challenges. They often rely on third-party providers for credit card processing. However, for this to work, the third-party provider must have a partnering bank prepared to accept deposits from them. This is a crucial aspect in servicing these high-risk industries, but one that the third-party providers usually accomplish, albeit with some restrictions imposed by the bank.

The Gap: Cannabis Industry Challenges

Beyond that, there are sectors that major banks and card networks avoid altogether. These businesses cannot access services from the bank directly or from the third-party providers that typically assist high-risk businesses. Third-party providers must be fully transparent with their partnering banks regarding the source of their deposits. If a bank declines to accept deposits from specific retail sectors, the third-party provider loses the ability to provide compliant services to those retailers.
